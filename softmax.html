1What softmax is

A neural network's last linear layer outputs raw scores called logits — any real numbers, positive or negative. Softmax turns that vector into a probability distribution: every entry lands in $(0,1)$ and the whole vector sums to $1$.

From scores to probabilities

If a classifier emits logits $\mathbf{z} = (z_1,\dots,z_K)$ for $K$ classes, softmax gives

$$\text{softmax}(\mathbf{z})_i \;=\; \frac{e^{z_i}}{\sum_{j=1}^{K} e^{z_j}}.$$

Inside a transformer it does the same job for the $K$ tokens an attention head looks at: turn raw similarity scores into normalized attention weights.

Why a soft max?

Hard $\arg\max$ kills the gradient — only one slot wins, and tiny changes in the loser scores produce zero learning signal. Softmax is a smooth, differentiable approximation: the largest logit dominates, but every logit still gets a (small) share of the probability mass and a usable gradient.

2The formula, derived

Construction

We want a function that takes any vector and produces nonnegative numbers summing to 1.

  1. Make scores positive: apply a strictly positive map. The natural exponential $e^{z}$ is the cleanest choice — smooth, monotonic, $e^z>0$ always.
  2. Normalize: divide each by the sum so the total is 1.
$$\text{softmax}(\mathbf{z})_i = \frac{\exp(z_i)}{\sum_j \exp(z_j)}.$$

Why exponential, not just $z_i^2$ or $|z_i|$?

  • Strictly positive for all real inputs (no zero denominators, no sign games).
  • Multiplicative gaps: $e^{z_i}/e^{z_j} = e^{z_i - z_j}$ depends only on differences. Adding a constant to every logit changes nothing — see §3.
  • Maximum-entropy: among distributions with a fixed mean energy $\mathbb{E}[z]$, the exponential family has highest entropy. Softmax is the maximally non-committal distribution consistent with the logits.
  • Conjugate to log-likelihood: paired with cross-entropy the gradient collapses to a single line — see §6.

Three properties to internalize

  • Sums to 1. By construction: numerator sums = denominator. Drag any slider — the bars always fill the row.
  • Monotonic in each input. Increase $z_i$ and $p_i$ goes up (others go down). Never crosses.
  • Shift-invariance. $\text{softmax}(\mathbf{z}+c\mathbf{1}) = \text{softmax}(\mathbf{z})$. The numerator and denominator both pick up a factor $e^c$ which cancels. This is the foundation of the numerical stability trick: pick $c = -\max_i z_i$ so the largest exponent is $0$ — no overflow, no information loss.

Try the Apply log-sum-exp trick button: it shifts logits by $-\max$. Bars don't move (shift-invariance!) but the visible exponentials are now all $\le 1$.

4Temperature

τ scales logits

$$\text{softmax}_\tau(\mathbf{z})_i = \frac{e^{z_i/\tau}}{\sum_j e^{z_j/\tau}}.$$
  • $\tau \to \infty$: every $z_i/\tau \to 0$, distribution becomes uniform $1/K$. Maximum exploration.
  • $\tau \to 0^{+}$: gaps blow up, the largest logit wins, distribution collapses to a one-hot at $\arg\max$. Pure greedy.
  • $\tau = 1$: standard softmax.

In LLM sampling, temperature is exactly this knob: high $\tau$ produces diverse, surprising tokens; low $\tau$ produces deterministic, repetitive ones.

Try it

Slide τ in the widget above between $0.05$ and $5$:

  • τ small → bar of the winner pushes toward 1.0
  • τ = 1 → ordinary softmax
  • τ large → bars flatten toward $1/3 \approx 0.333$

Annealing — gradually lowering τ during training or sampling — is a classic move borrowed from statistical physics, where $\tau$ literally is temperature in the Boltzmann distribution.

5Why softmax is in attention

Scaled dot-product attention

$$\text{Attn}(Q,K,V) = \text{softmax}\!\left(\frac{QK^\top}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right) V.$$

For a query token, $QK^\top$ produces a similarity score against every key. Softmax converts those scores into attention weights: nonnegative, summing to 1, one per source token.

The output is then $\sum_j w_j v_j$ — a weighted average of value vectors. Because $\sum w_j = 1$ the magnitude of the output stays comparable to a single $v_j$, no matter how many tokens we attend over. That's why softmax (not, say, ReLU normalization) is the default.

Why $1/\sqrt{d_k}$ before softmax?

If queries and keys have unit-variance components, $QK^\top$ has variance $\propto d_k$. With large $d_k$ the logits get huge, softmax saturates, gradients vanish. Dividing by $\sqrt{d_k}$ keeps the variance $\approx 1$ — softmax stays in its sensitive, learnable regime. This is essentially a temperature of $\tau = \sqrt{d_k}$.

6Softmax + cross-entropy: the gradient

Setup

Let $p_i = \text{softmax}(z)_i$, target one-hot $y$, cross-entropy

$$L = -\sum_i y_i \log p_i.$$

We want $\partial L/\partial z_i$. The Jacobian of softmax is messy:

$$\frac{\partial p_i}{\partial z_j} = p_i(\delta_{ij} - p_j).$$

But composed with $L$, things collapse beautifully.

The collapse

Chain rule:

$$\frac{\partial L}{\partial z_i} = \sum_k \frac{\partial L}{\partial p_k}\frac{\partial p_k}{\partial z_i} = \sum_k \left(-\frac{y_k}{p_k}\right)\, p_k(\delta_{ki}-p_i).$$

Simplify:

$$=\;-\sum_k y_k(\delta_{ki}-p_i) \;=\; -y_i + p_i\sum_k y_k \;=\; p_i - y_i.$$

So $\nabla_{z}L = \mathbf{p} - \mathbf{y}$. The gradient is the error vector: predicted minus target. Clean, cheap, numerically friendly. This is why softmax + cross-entropy are paired everywhere.

7Numerical stability — the log-sum-exp trick

Computing $e^{1000}$ overflows 64-bit floats; computing $e^{-1000}$ underflows to $0$ and $\log 0 = -\infty$. Naive softmax is fragile. Shift-invariance saves us.

Let $m = \max_i z_i$. Then

$$\text{softmax}(\mathbf{z})_i = \frac{e^{z_i - m}}{\sum_j e^{z_j - m}}.$$

Now the largest exponent is $e^0 = 1$; everything else is in $(0,1]$. No overflow, smallest possible roundoff.

For $\log\,\text{softmax}$ the log-sum-exp identity is the canonical form:

$$\log\sum_j e^{z_j} = m + \log\sum_j e^{z_j - m}.$$

Used in every framework's log_softmax, nll_loss, and attention kernel.

8Alternatives, briefly

Sparsemax

Projects logits onto the probability simplex. Output is genuinely sparse — exact zeros are allowed. Useful when you want attention to ignore most tokens entirely (interpretability, structured prediction). Not as smooth, requires a sorting step, but differentiable almost everywhere.

Gumbel-softmax

Adds Gumbel noise then takes softmax with low temperature: a continuous, differentiable relaxation of sampling from a categorical. Lets you train discrete latent variables with backprop (e.g. discrete VAEs, hard attention).

Other relatives (entmax-1.5, spherical softmax, mixture of softmaxes…)

Entmax-α interpolates between softmax (α=1) and sparsemax (α=2). Mixture-of-softmaxes addresses the “softmax bottleneck” in language modeling. All trade some property — sparsity, expressiveness, compute — against vanilla softmax.
